1. Problem Setting

What exact problem is the paper trying to solve? Define the task carefully. Is it integration, prediction, alignment, generation, segmentation, prognosis, perturbation modeling, or something else?

2. Data and Inputs

Data type

e.g. histopathology, RNA-seq, ATAC-seq, methylation, WSI, spatial transcriptomics

Granularity

e.g. patient-level, slide-level, patch-level, cell-level, spot-level

Scale

Number of samples, number of patients, number of genes, number of images, cohorts, institutions

Supervision

Paired or unpaired, weak labels or strong labels, clinical endpoints, pseudo-labels

3. Core Method

Explain the method in your own words. What is the model architecture? What are the main modules? What is the objective function really encouraging the model to do?

  • Main model family: CNN, Transformer, GNN, VAE, flow, diffusion, MIL, ODE, etc.
  • Key inductive bias: what structure is assumed?
  • Loss terms: reconstruction, contrastive, classification, ranking, survival, regularization
  • What is genuinely new, and what is inherited from previous work?

4. Main Results

Record the most important experimental findings only. Which baselines are compared? Which metric matters most? Are the gains large, stable, and biologically meaningful, or just incremental?

5. Strengths

  • What is elegant or convincing in the formulation?
  • What experimental design choices are particularly strong?
  • What makes the paper useful for your own research?

6. Weaknesses and Questions

  • Which assumptions seem fragile?
  • What biological or clinical gap remains unresolved?
  • What would you challenge if you were reviewing this paper?

7. Relation to My Research

This is the most important part. Write how the paper connects to your own work, for example MORN, computational pathology, survival modeling, multimodal representation learning, or mechanistic interpretability.

8. Possible Follow-up Ideas

  • Which idea can be reused directly?
  • Which limitation suggests a new method direction?
  • What experiment would you run if you wanted to extend this work?
