Multi-omics Integration

This section focuses on methods for aligning or jointly modeling multiple omics modalities. The main emphasis is on how papers handle paired versus unpaired settings, what assumptions they make about shared information, and whether their learned representations support biologically meaningful downstream tasks.

Question
What Is Shared?
Whether the method explicitly separates shared biological signal from modality-specific variation.
Question
How Is Matching Done?
Anchors, latent alignment, contrastive learning, optimal transport, or probabilistic modeling.
Question
What Is Evaluated?
Alignment quality, cross-modal prediction, batch robustness, and biological conservation.

Paper List

PNAS 2026 Single-cell Integration Statistical Learning

Bridging Unpaired Single-cell Multimodal Data for Integrative Analyses with SuperMap

The paper proposes a statistical framework for multi-omics alignment. Specifically, the ATAC modality is used to infer the unobserved RNA modality, while a distributional constraint encourages the inferred RNA to follow a distribution similar to the observed RNA.

Browse
NeurIPS 2025 Single-cell Integration VAE

scMRDR: A Scalable and Flexible Framework for Unpaired Single-Cell Multi-Omics Data Integration

The paper proposes a β-VAE-based disentanglement framework that decomposes cellular representations into modality-shared and modality-specific latent components, and evaluates the method on biological conservation, batch correction, and modality integration tasks.

Trajectory Inference

This section follows papers that model cellular dynamics from single-cell and multi-omic data. The key interest is not only whether a method reconstructs trajectories, but also whether it provides interpretable temporal structure, lineage-specific behavior, or mechanistic insight into regulatory changes.

Focus
Temporal Structure
Pseudotime, latent time, trajectory consistency, and lineage-aware progression.
Focus
Regulatory Kinetics
How the method links chromatin, transcription, or multimodal state transitions over time.
Focus
Interpretability
Whether the model explains dynamic programs rather than only producing embeddings.
